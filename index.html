MY STORY

Eradicate that resentments and guilt and thinking about the 'If's'.

I have been attempting to do one thing in my life and that is to “BE HAPPY”. Alcoholism and depression was my life for almost 7 years SINCE the year 2006 and it basically led me to severe liver cirrhosis and “almost” certain death.

I had to deal with the most painful moment in my life and the fact that the liver consultant mentioned for the fact that I was in a grave condition and might not pull through the next few weeks and I was “hanging by a thread.” It daunted upon me at that time firstly, I hadn't much time to live;

Secondly, I haven't done the things I wanted to and was in a despondent state. Tears welled up in my eyes as I realised the possibility that I wasn't going to see my son grow up. I was completely devastated, defeated and destroyed. Any visitors I had just gave me that sort of pitiful gaze and the look of despair and hopelessness. If there was anything I ever knew about the society I lived; was one of a selfish and non-compassionate society for alcoholics. It had almost seemed to me that I might not be given a second chance at life.

Time stood still, and every second seemed a lifetime. I had 2 choices. Stay and Rot or get out of the hospital. I had 2 solutions, decrease my bilirubin toxic levels and get out and carry on feeling sorry with myself. Either way I ran the risk of dying. I contemplated the former. 1 week later I moved out of hospital with a decrease in my toxic levels.

I still had the support of my son and my wonderful wife. I still had hope. It was all I ever needed. I leveraged on the support I got to get back on my feet and stopped all forms of medications.

I live in a society that focuses on results and I realised I could not conform to it. I had to change my attitude towards my life in to pull away from the dreaded misery I was in. I honestly never believed in therapy as I never believed it was going to work for me; and yet here's the most surprising thing for me after 10 years,

  1. Have been sober
  2. have managed to control my anxiety (in most cases)
  3. I have redefined my idea of success
  4. I am trying to let go my perceived perception of they way people perceive me (it isn't a pun)
  5. I have almost eradicated the “3 deadly sins” - (in my own opinion) from without any hard-fast methodologies or rules and protocols.
  • Anger -that's a tough one
  • Being judgmental - That's another tough one
  • Guilt - oh that kills most of us leading us to lie not just others but to oursellves which automatically leads to shame -shame of past actions and its repercussions, debts, vices etc..

All these led to myself losing track of who I really am. It just made me feel extremely small inside.

So….. the BIG QUESTION, How did I pull it off?

That's where you get to meet me at the live sessions that I would be having to explain the concept of 2W's and yes my acronym which constitutes to my name S.R.E.E.
